Postpartum Depression Symptom Checker

Assess potential symptoms of postpartum depression and find support resources

If you're in crisis: This is not a diagnostic tool. If you're experiencing postpartum depression symptoms, contact the National Mental Health Helpline at 14416 or 1800-891-4416 for confidential support. You're not alone.

1. Do you feel sad, hopeless, or overwhelmed most of the day?

2. Have you lost interest or pleasure in activities you used to enjoy?

3. Do you have trouble bonding with your baby?

4. Do you feel excessively anxious about your baby's health or safety?

5. Do you have trouble sleeping (even when your baby is sleeping) or sleep too much?

6. Do you have changes in appetite (eating much more or much less than usual)?

7. Do you feel unusually irritable or angry?

8. Do you feel exhausted or have no energy, even after resting?

9. Do you feel worthless, guilty, or like you're failing as a mother?

10. Do you have trouble concentrating, making decisions, or remembering things?

11. Have you had thoughts about harming yourself or your baby?

12. Do you feel like you would be better off dead or have thoughts of suicide?
